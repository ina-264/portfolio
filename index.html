Designing a Seamless 'Order Status' Feature

Order Anywhere — Lightspeed Commerce

Role Lead Product Designer
Timeline 4 months (Mar–Jul 2022)

Project Overview

The Order Anywhere platform lacked an intuitive way to communicate order status for table orders and pickups. Customers were frustrated with limited updates via email, leading to uncertainty and frequent inquiries. The new Order Status feature provides customers with clear, estimated order updates directly through the platform, enhancing transparency and confidence in the ordering process.

Key Goals

  • Provide clear order updates to reduce uncertainty.
  • Enable users to track their order status via intuitive visual indicators.
  • Improve operational efficiency by dynamically aligning updates with merchant-entered data.

Why This Matters: Empowering customers with timely updates builds trust, enhances the ordering experience, and drives satisfaction.

Problem Statement

Customers struggled with uncertainty about their order status due to vague email updates that were often delayed or overlooked. This lack of transparency led to customer dissatisfaction and operational inefficiencies, with staff handling frequent inquiries about order readiness.

How Might We

How might we design an intuitive, estimate-based Order Status feature that provides clarity and transparency for customers and improves restaurant operational efficiency?

Research

To ensure a consumer-centered design process, the following methodologies were employed:

01

Semi-Structured Interviews

Conducted one-on-one interviews with 10 participants representing regular users of table ordering and pickup services. Allowed for in-depth exploration of user pain points, behaviors, and expectations while providing flexibility to probe specific areas of interest.

02

Behavioral Analysis

Observed user interaction patterns with existing order status systems through contextual scenarios. Helped identify gaps in current solutions, such as delayed email notifications or unclear status indicators, and provided insights into natural user behavior.

Key Insights

  • Frustration with Uncertainty: Users felt anxious about not knowing when their orders would be ready, particularly during busy hours. Many cited that delayed or missed email notifications worsened their experience.
  • Preference for In-Platform Updates: Participants preferred receiving updates directly on the ordering platform rather than relying on email. Notifications with estimated timelines were favored to reduce inquiries and provide clarity.
  • Simplicity and Transparency: Clear visual indicators, such as progress bars or status labels, were widely requested. Users valued concise, approachable language like "approx. 15 minutes" to manage expectations.

Competitor Analysis

This SWOT analysis focuses on the order status feature for it is critical to customer satisfaction, operational efficiency, and competitive differentiation in the food delivery market.

User Flow Mapping

Mapped the user flow for table orders and pickups, focusing on:

  • Order Confirmation: Customer receives estimated time for preparation.
  • Preparation Stage: Progress bar updates based on milestones.
  • Ready for Pickup/Table: Clear notification when the order is ready.

How Research Shaped Design Goals

  • Semi-structured interviews revealed that vague and delayed updates were the biggest source of dissatisfaction, leading to the incorporation of clear, in-platform progress indicators.
  • Behavioral analysis highlighted the need for intuitive interfaces that provide quick access to updates without requiring excessive navigation.
  • Competitor benchmarking underscored the importance of visual feedback and managing expectations through language like "approx. 10–15 minutes" to foster user confidence.

User Testing

Objective: To validate the Order Status feature's usability, focusing on navigation, clarity of estimated timelines, and overall user satisfaction.

Methodology

  • Participants: 8 tech-savvy users familiar with table ordering and pickup platforms.
  • Tasks: Locate order status, interpret progress indicators, and respond to notifications.
  • Tools: Conducted via Maze, capturing qualitative and quantitative feedback.

Key Findings

  • Progress Indicators: Provided clarity on order stages, reducing user anxiety.
  • Estimated Timelines: Managed expectations with approximate times, though users requested brief explanations for accuracy.
  • Notifications: Timely in-platform updates were well-received; as well as email notifications for critical updates.
  • Navigation: Intuitive design made status easy to locate, with suggestions for tooltips to help first-time users.

Project Outcomes

The redesigned Order Status feature achieved significant improvements:

  • Improved Transparency: Customers gained clear visibility into their order progress through intuitive progress indicators and estimated timelines.
  • Reduced Inquiries: Proactive updates significantly minimized customer questions about order readiness.
  • Enhanced User Satisfaction: Users appreciated the clarity, simplicity, and timeliness of the new Order Status feature.

Reflections

This project highlighted the importance of aligning user needs with clear, actionable design solutions. By emphasizing transparency and simplicity, we addressed key frustrations and improved the overall ordering experience. Future iterations could explore predictive notifications and optional push alerts to further enhance usability and customer satisfaction.

A Self-Delivery System for Order Anywhere

Order Anywhere — Lightspeed Commerce

Role Lead Product Designer
Timeline 6 months (Jun–Nov 2023)

Project Overview

Third-party delivery services often impose high costs and limit restaurants' control over operations, negatively impacting profit margins and customer experiences. This project aimed to design a self-delivery feature for Order Anywhere, enabling restaurants to independently manage their deliveries while offering customers personalized and flexible delivery options, such as saved addresses and scheduled deliveries.

Key Goals

  • Reduce Costs: Enable restaurants to eliminate reliance on third-party platforms and retain more revenue.
  • Improve Customer Experience: Provide tailored delivery options, such as saved addresses and scheduling.
  • Increase Control: Allow restaurants to define delivery ranges, fees, and timelines for consistent service.
  • Simplify Use: Ensure the system is intuitive for both restaurant staff and customers.

By addressing these pain points, the self-delivery feature strengthens restaurant profitability, builds trust with customers, and creates a seamless ordering experience.

Problem Statement

Restaurants relying on third-party delivery services face significant challenges that erode profitability and customer trust. Customers, in turn, encounter inconsistent service and lack the personalized options — such as saved addresses and flexible scheduling — that modern diners expect.

How Might We

How might we create an intuitive and efficient self-delivery feature that empowers restaurant owners to manage deliveries while enhancing customer convenience with personalized options and flexible scheduling?

Research

To design a self-delivery feature tailored to both restaurant and customer needs, we employed the following research methods:

01

Stakeholder Interviews

Restaurant owners and managers. Identified operational challenges with third-party platforms, including high fees, lack of delivery control, and customer dissatisfaction.

02

Customer Surveys

60 frequent users of online food ordering platforms. Explored preferences for delivery features, such as saved addresses, scheduling, and communication directly from restaurants.

Key Findings

  • Restaurant Insights: High fees from third-party platforms heavily impact profits. Restaurants need greater autonomy over delivery timelines, fees, and customer interactions.
  • Customer Insights: 70% wanted saved address functionality for faster checkout. 65% valued flexible delivery scheduling to suit their availability. 80% preferred direct updates from the restaurant instead of relying on third-party systems.

Competitor Analysis

Leading platforms such as Uber Eats and DoorDash were evaluated to uncover best practices and identify gaps in delivery workflows and customer experience, informing the design of a more tailored and efficient solution.

User Flow Mapping

Mapped the user journey for both restaurant staff and consumers, ensuring seamless integration and clarity:

  • Restaurant Staff Workflow: Designed the process for managing self-delivery, including enabling the feature, setting delivery ranges, pricing, and providing status updates.
  • Consumer Experience Flow: Focused on the customer's journey from order placement to delivery, emphasizing features like saved addresses, scheduling, and status notifications.

User Testing

Consumer-Side Testing

Methods:

  • Task-Based Testing: 10 consumers familiar with online food delivery platforms. Scenarios included saving addresses for future orders, scheduling deliveries for specific times, and switching between pickup and delivery options.
  • Think-Aloud Protocol: Participants verbalized their thoughts while interacting with the prototype to identify pain points and validate usability.

Key Findings:

  • Ease of Navigation: 90% of participants completed tasks smoothly, praising the intuitive design.
  • Saved Addresses: 75% found this feature significantly reduced checkout time and improved convenience.
  • Estimated Timelines: Users valued approximate delivery times but wanted explanations for accuracy (e.g., "based on current workload").

Restaurant Staff Testing

Methods:

  • Task-Based Testing: 6 restaurant staff members, including managers and delivery coordinators. Scenarios included enabling self-delivery, setting delivery ranges, adjusting delivery times dynamically, and managing notifications.
  • Observation Sessions: Monitored staff interactions with the prototype in simulated workflow scenarios to assess operational fit and ease of use.

Key Findings:

  • Feature Enablement: Staff appreciated the straightforward process for enabling self-delivery and configuring settings.
  • Delivery Configuration: The ability to set custom delivery ranges, prices, and estimated times provided flexibility, but some staff suggested tooltips for first-time users.
  • Ease of Use: 85% found the interface simple and intuitive, reducing the need for extensive training.
  • Dynamic Adjustments: Real-time adjustment options for delivery times were praised, especially during peak hours.

Project Outcomes

The self-delivery feature addressed key challenges, enabling restaurants to reduce third-party reliance, retain more revenue, and streamline operations. Consumers benefited from saved addresses, flexible scheduling, and real-time updates, enhancing convenience and satisfaction. Restaurant staff gained tools for managing delivery settings and timelines efficiently.

Reflections

This project underscored the importance of designing for both operational efficiency and user convenience. Flexible delivery tools and clear communication features were key to its success. Future iterations could explore predictive timelines and enhanced analytics for further optimization.

Revamping POS Notifications

POS System — Lightspeed Commerce

Role Lead Product Designer
Timeline 5 months (Dec 2021–Apr 2022)

Project Overview

In the fast-paced service industry, effective communication is critical for seamless operations. Lightspeed POS's notification system faced challenges in discoverability and usability, resulting in missed alerts for critical issues like printer errors and connectivity disruptions.

To address these issues, we redesigned the notification system to provide:

  • Improved Visibility: A prominent notification icon for real-time alerts.
  • Clear Messaging: Simplified, actionable notifications.
  • Interactive Elements: Direct-action options for quick issue resolution.

Why This Matters: Enhancing the notification system empowers users, improves operational efficiency, and positions Lightspeed POS as a trusted tool in high-pressure environments.

Problem Statement

The notification system failed to support users effectively due to:

  • Low Discoverability: Users were unaware of its existence.
  • Ambiguous Icons: Confusing iconography delayed actions.
  • Missed Alerts: Critical operational issues went unnoticed.
  • Inefficient Workflows: Users struggled to respond to issues promptly, leading to downtime and service disruptions.

How Might We

How might we design an intuitive and actionable notification system that ensures critical alerts are easily discovered, understood, and acted upon to enhance operational efficiency in high-pressure environments?

Research

User Research

To understand user pain points and needs, I employed the following methods:

01

Contextual Inquiry

Observed users interacting with the POS system during peak operations, uncovering their challenges and decision-making processes.

02

Stakeholder Interviews

Collaborated with restaurant managers, service staff, and technical teams to understand workflows and technical constraints.

03

Diary Studies

Collected data from users over a week, documenting their frustrations, successes, and emotional responses to the current notification system.

Key Insights

  • Users frequently missed alerts due to poor visibility and unclear messaging.
  • Service staff required actionable notifications to resolve issues quickly.
  • Restaurant workflows varied significantly, necessitating a flexible design.

Market Research

The global POS market, valued at $11.99 billion in 2022, is growing at a CAGR of 10.8%. Trends emphasize real-time updates, cloud-based solutions, and user-friendly systems. These insights highlighted the need for clear, accessible notification systems to improve operational efficiency.

Personas

User Flow Mapping

  • Actionability and Prioritization: Restaurant owners highlighted the importance of categorization and actionable alerts for effective operational management.
  • Ease of Use: Hotel bar staff inspired simplified language and intuitive designs to accommodate varying levels of technical expertise and experience.

User Testing

  • Increased Satisfaction: Positive feedback validated the design's impact.

Reflections

By focusing on discoverability, clarity, and actionability, we created a notification system that improved efficiency and reduced frustration. Key takeaways include the value of iterative testing and cross-functional collaboration in aligning technical constraints with user needs. The solution's success demonstrates how thoughtful design can streamline workflows, enhance satisfaction, and build trust. Future iterations could include predictive notifications and customizable settings to further support evolving user workflows and needs.

02 / How I Work Design process

Process

Research, framing, design, iteration — in that order. The quality of the outcome depends on the quality of the decisions made early.

Research & insight

I start by listening — to users, to data, to the gaps between what people say and what they do. Research isn't a deliverable. It's how I figure out which question is actually worth answering.

Problem framing

Before any interface, I spend time making the problem legible. Misframing the problem makes every design decision downstream fragile. Getting this right is where most of the real value is.

Design & iteration

I design for clarity and usability, not novelty. Flows get simplified until they make sense. Copy gets pressure-tested. Edge cases get considered. I refine until the experience holds up under realistic conditions.

Selective AI acceleration

AI earns time back on the parts that don't need my judgment — generating variants, moving through scaffolding, structuring documentation. That time goes back into research and decisions.
